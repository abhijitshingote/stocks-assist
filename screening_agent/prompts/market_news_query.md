# Primary hunting grounds (look here FIRST)

These are the catalyst types most likely to produce real themes. Bias your
search toward them:

1. **Earnings-driven cycle inflections** — a bellwether company prints
   numbers (or guides) that re-rate an entire sub-sector from "cyclical
   beta" to "secular grower", or kicks off a new supercycle inside a
   bigger theme. Recent earnings calendar bellwethers to check:
   semiconductors (Intel, AMD, Micron, Broadcom, AVGO, TSM, ASML,
   Marvell, KLAC, AMAT), AI infrastructure (NVDA, ARM, ANET, VRT, ETN,
   GEV, EMR), software (NOW, CRM, ORCL, SNOW, DDOG, CRWD, PANW),
   biotech/pharma (LLY, NVO, AMGN, REGN, VRTX), industrials
   (CAT, DE, ETN, ITW), energy services (SLB, HAL, BKR), payments
   (V, MA, PYPL, FI). Don't restrict to these — but actively check
   whether any bellwether's recent print kicked off a new narrative.
2. **Supply / demand structural shifts** — a sector moves from
   spot/cyclical pricing to multi-quarter contracted pricing
   (memory, gas turbines, transformers, peptide/GLP-1 fill-finish,
   uranium, TCE shipping rates).
3. **New product / technology cycle hitting commercialization** — first
   real revenue prints for things like silicon photonics, custom ASICs,
   solid-state batteries, eVTOL, CRISPR, robotaxis.
4. **Policy / regulatory catalysts that unlock a sector** — government
   contracts, FDA actions, tariff / quota changes, IRA-style funding
   awards. Must be a SPECIFIC announcement, not "policy uncertainty".
5. **Fresh activist / management actions signaling thesis change** —
   insider buys at scale, big buyback announcements, capex pivots,
   CEO transitions tied to a strategic re-rating.

# What I do NOT want

Hard-reject. If your draft list contains these, delete and find better:

- Geopolitics / war / oil-shock narratives (unless paired with a specific
  equity re-rating thesis).
- Macro / rates / inflation chatter ("sticky inflation", "10-year at X",
  "Fed hike odds", "ECB on hold").
- Broad index statistics ("74% of S&P beat", "median EPS growth +N%",
  "best earnings season since...").
- Positioning / sentiment chatter ("Mag 7 caution", "rotation out of
  tech", "max bullish unwind").
- Tape-watching ("utilities only 6% above the 20-day", "small caps
  lagging").
- Recycled evergreen themes with no fresh catalyst.
- Promo-style "growth stock pick" listicles where the only "catalyst"
  is "this stock appears in our screener".
